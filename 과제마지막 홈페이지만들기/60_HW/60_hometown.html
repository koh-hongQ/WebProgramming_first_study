    History of Seocho-gu
    Origin of the Name "Seocho-gu"


    During the Joseon Dynasty, parts of Yeomgok-dong, Shinwon-dong, Naegok-dong, and Yangjae-dong were in Gwangju-gun, and the remaining area was in Gwacheon-gun. In 1914, Gwacheon-gun was integrated into Siheung-gun. Until the 1960s, the area was mostly rice fields. It is also the background to the brutal history of Maljukgeori, set in Gangnam Eunkwang Girls’ High School and Sangmun High School. In 1963, Sindong-myeon, Siheung-gun, Gyeonggi-do, was incorporated into Yeongdeungpo-gu, and Eonju-myeon, Gwangju-gun, was incorporated into Seongdong-gu. Among the former Sindong-myeon areas, the Dongjak-daero area, excluding Bangbae-dong, was incorporated into Seongdong-gu in 1973 following the establishment of Gwanak-gu. Subsequently, in 1975, the entire area south of the Han River in Seongdong-gu, including the current Seocho-gu, was divided into Gangnam-gu.
